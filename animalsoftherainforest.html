Animals of the Rainforest

Here are some examples of animals from the millions of species in the rainforest:
  • The Okapi
    The okapi, which is also known as the forest giraffe, Congolese giraffe, or zebra giraffe, is an mammal native to the northeast of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and the rainforest. Although the okapi has striped markings reminiscent of zebras, it is most closely related to the giraffe. The okapi and the giraffe are the only living members of the family Giraffidae, yet their habitat is disappearing. They are threatened for extinction.

  • Black Howler Monkies howl to mark their territory. The howling sounds like strong wind blowing through a tunnel. Their howls can be heard from up to 2 miles away. They live in groups of 4 up to 19. They live in high up rainforest trees. They sleep for 70% of the day. They can live up to 20 years. They eat mostly leaves and occasionally figs. Female howler monkies are light brown.
